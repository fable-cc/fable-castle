## Thread 1: Why your father never said "I'm proud of you"

1/ My father has probably praised me five times in my entire life. Maybe less.

2/ It's not that he isn't proud. It's that nobody ever taught his generation how to express it. Their love language is: "Have you eaten?" "Do you need money?" "Go to sleep early."

3/ But here's what I didn't realize until recently: his father never praised HIM either. He's never been praised. He doesn't know what it feels like. So he can't give what he never received.

4/ Confucianism says "父子有亲" — the bond between father and son isn't blood. It's connection. Connection requires someone to reach first. He can't. You can.

5/ Tonight. Call him. Don't say "why don't you ever praise me." Say: "Dad, I was thinking — was grandpa really strict with you when you were young?" Silence. Then he'll say things he's never told anyone.

## Thread 2: You're not attracting the wrong person — you're selecting them

1/ "I always end up with the wrong person." Three relationships. Same ending.

2/ Adler would shake his head. You're not "attracting" them. You're SELECTING them. You only feel chemistry with people who feel "familiar." Familiar ≠ good. Familiar = what you grew up with.

3/ If you watched your mother give everything and get nothing back — your brain learned: love = sacrifice without return. A stable, giving partner feels "off." An emotionally distant one feels "right."

4/ The fix: next time you feel instant chemistry — pause. Ask: "Am I attracted because they're good for me, or because they feel familiar?" If it's the second — you have a choice. Not to choose them. To stop repeating.

## Thread 3: Why you can't charge for your work

1/ Someone asks: "How much do I owe you?" You say: "Don't worry about it."

2/ You said that not because you're generous. You said it because you don't know what you're worth. Name a price too high — they think you're greedy. Too low — they think you're cheap. Say nothing — safest.

3/ The hidden equation in your head: "Charging money = I guarantee your satisfaction." You're terrified they'll pay and then feel it wasn't worth it. So you work for free. Free protects you from being judged as "not worth it."

4/ But free has a cost: uncommitted clients. They ask for endless revisions. They don't value your time. They move on without a second thought. Paying clients self-select. They're serious. You do better work. Both sides win.

5/ You don't need to figure out "what I'm worth." Pick a price. ¥39.9. If someone pays — you're worth it. Nobody pays — adjust. Three tries. You'll find your number.

## Thread 4: Your kids don't listen to you — they watch you

1/ You tell your kid: "Put down the phone." You're holding yours. "Read more." You don't read. "Don't yell." You're yelling.

2/ You think you're parenting. You're not. They don't hear what you say. They see what you do.

3/ Laozi called this "行不言之教" — teaching without words. The most effective education. Not "stop scrolling" — you stop first. Not "go read" — you pick up a book. Not "calm down" — you take a breath.

4/ Change one habit today. Not for yourself. For them. So they don't spend 30 years unlearning what you accidentally taught them.

## Thread 5: The real reason you procrastinate

1/ You've been putting something off for three months. It's not that you're lazy. It's that every time you think about starting — you open your phone instead.

2/ Here's why: procrastination isn't laziness. It's fear. Fear that when you finish, you'll discover "this is the best I can do — and it's not that good."

3/ Not starting protects you. As long as you haven't started, you can still be "someone who could be great." Starting means facing the gap between who you imagine you could be and who you actually are.

4/ The fix: make the task so small you're embarrassed NOT to do it. Not "write an article" — "open a document and type one sentence." Do step one. Your brain can't stay in "protection mode" and "execution mode" at the same time. Step one breaks the loop.

## Thread 6: How to start building your personal brand

1/ "I want to build a personal brand but I don't know where to start." Most common question I get.

2/ Answer: stop thinking. Post 30 things first. You can't figure out your niche by thinking. You figure it out by doing. Post 30 pieces — any direction. Look back. Which 5 performed best? That's your niche.

3/ When I started, my first post was terrible. So was my tenth. Around post 100, people started DMing me. By post 600, clients came to me. I didn't figure out my direction first — I found it by doing.

4/ You don't know if you're good enough. You won't know until you start. And "thinking about starting" doesn't count. Today. Post one thing. Doesn't have to be perfect. Just has to be posted.

## Thread 8: Strong culture vs weak culture

1/ Chinese philosophy has a concept that changed my life: 强势文化 (strong culture) vs 弱势文化 (weak culture).

2/ Weak culture: waiting. Waiting for a better boss. Waiting for someone to recognize you. Waiting for the "right time." You tell yourself you're being patient. You're not. You're being controlled — by the things you're waiting for.

3/ Strong culture: starting before you're ready. Not because you're confident. Because you understand readiness never comes. Nobody felt fully prepared for their first business. Their first speech. Their first risk.

4/ One action today: pick one thing you've been waiting for permission to do. Start it. Not because you're ready. Because you're done waiting.
